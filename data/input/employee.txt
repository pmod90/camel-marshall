with 3D MS data, called MaxPlot.</p>\n<p>You may recall that we learned about MaxPlot for 3D PDA data in <a href=\"http://blog.waters.com/get-empowered-review-window-and-the-processing-method-tip-52\">Tip 52</a>. Today we are going to learn about MaxPlot for 3D MS data. MaxPlot is a 2D chromatogram that plots each time point at the maximum intensity across the mass range. MaxPlot is Empower&#8217;s term for the channel of data commonly referred to in mass spectrometry as the BPI (Base Peak Intensity) channel. It uses only the intensity of the most intense peak (base peak) at a given retention time, contrary to the TIC which takes all ions into account.</p>\n<p>Let me show you how it is done.</p>\n<h3>Step
